Formic acid, also known as methanoic acid, is a simple carboxylic acid that has a wide range of applications across various industries. Its primary uses include serving as a preservative and antibacterial agent in animal feed, as well as a key intermediate in the production of chemicals like acetic acid and formate salts. It is also utilized in the leather, textile, and rubber industries for purposes such as tanning, dyeing, and processing. Formic acid has been gaining popularity in recent years due to its versatile nature and eco-friendly properties. As the demand for more sustainable chemical solutions grows, formic acid is increasingly considered an attractive alternative to harsher chemicals in several industrial applications.
